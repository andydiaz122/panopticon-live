1. Orientation — the CapCut UI

CapCut Desktop has 4 main panels:

PanelWhereWhat it's for
Media BinTop-leftYour imported assets. Drag from here onto the timeline.
PreviewTop-rightPlays whatever your timeline cursor is parked on.
PropertiesRight side, below previewEdit selected clip's speed, color, volume, etc.
TimelineBottom (full width)The actual edit surface. Tracks stack vertically; time flows left-to-right.

Tracks numbered 1, 2, 3, 4 stack from bottom to top. Track 1 is the bottom layer; higher tracks render over lower ones. For our demo:

  • Track 4 (top): Title cards + text overlays
  • Track 3: OBS captures + intro typing video
  • Track 2: Music bed
  • Track 1 (bottom): Native intro audio (from intro_typing_v1.mov, separated)
Mental model

Think of the timeline like a stack of glass sheets. The TOP track is the visible visual; lower tracks are heard but not seen (audio) or hidden behind the top track (visual layers). Audio mixes additively across all audio tracks.

2. Project setup (60 seconds)

  1. Open CapCut. If a project is open from last night, use that — your assets are already imported.
  2. If starting fresh: File → New Project → name it panopticon_live_demo_v1
  3. Click the gear icon top-right (Project Settings)
  4. Set Resolution: 1920 × 1080
  5. Set Frame Rate: 60 fps (or 30 if 60 isn't an option)
  6. Set Aspect Ratio: 16:9
  7. Click Save
  8. Cmd+S to save the project file
If stuck Can't find Project Settings? Click the project name in the top-left corner — sometimes Settings lives there in older CapCut versions. Or look for "Canvas Size" / "Output Size" in any menu — same thing.

4. Dragging clips to the timeline

  1. Click and drag any clip from Media Bin down onto Track 3 (the visual track)
  2. Release. The clip lands at wherever you dropped it on the timeline.
  3. To position it precisely: click the clip and drag horizontally to slide it left/right
  4. To make a clip START at exactly 0:00: drag it all the way to the left edge of the timeline
Pro tip

CapCut snaps clips to nearby clip edges automatically. If you drag a clip near another clip's end, they'll click together. This is good — it eliminates 1-frame gaps that show up as black flashes during playback.

5. Trimming clips (start + end edges)

This is the operation you'll use most. Trimming SHORTENS a clip by cutting off content from the start or end. The remaining content plays at normal speed.

  1. Click a clip on the timeline — its edges become draggable handles
  2. Drag the LEFT edge inward (rightward) to cut off the BEGINNING of the clip
  3. Drag the RIGHT edge inward (leftward) to cut off the END of the clip
  4. The Preview pane updates as you drag — use it to find the exact frame where you want to cut
  5. Release when you've found the cut point
Watch this carefully

When you trim, CapCut may push other clips to the right to fill the gap (Ripple Edit) OR leave a gap (Standard Trim). Look at your timeline after each trim — if there's a black gap, drag the next clip leftward to close it. Black gaps = black flashes in the export.

If stuck Edges aren't draggable? Click the clip first to select it. Hover precisely over the very edge — your cursor should change to a horizontal arrow. If it doesn't, zoom in on the timeline (Cmd++ or scroll wheel) — small clips have thin edges that are hard to grab.

6. Splitting a clip mid-stream

Sometimes you don't want to trim the END of a clip — you want to KEEP both halves but treat them differently (e.g., speed-ramp the boring middle of a clip but keep the beginning + end at normal speed).

  1. Position the timeline cursor where you want to split (click on the time ruler at the top)
  2. Click the clip you want to split (so it's selected)
  3. Press S (the Split keyboard shortcut)
  4. The clip becomes TWO clips at that exact frame
  5. Now you can treat each half independently

Use case for our demo: the terminal precompute clip (2:25). You want the FIRST 2 seconds at normal speed (so viewers see what's happening), THEN speed-ramp the middle 2 minutes 20 seconds to ~3 seconds, THEN the LAST 1 second at normal speed (so the "Writing match_data.json ✓" line lands cleanly). Split the clip at 0:02 and 2:24, then speed-ramp only the middle piece.

7. Speed-ramping a clip

  1. Click a clip to select it
  2. In the Properties panel (right side), find the Speed tab
  3. OR right-click the clip → Speed → Custom
  4. Set the speed multiplier:
    • 0.5x = half speed (slow-mo)
    • 1.0x = normal
    • 2x – 4x = sped up, motion still visible
    • 10x – 50x = blur of fast motion (good for terminal precompute)
  5. Click Apply

Speed-ramp uses for our demo:

ClipSpeedWhy
terminal_precompute middle30x to 50xFast scrolling logs blur into "tech porn" without showing the wait
Intro typing portions1.4xAdds urgency without losing keystroke clarity
Tab 1 cold-open up to anomaly moment0.5x at the anomaly frame (35.9s, 45.3s, 59.1s)Cinematic broadcast slow-mo — your "explosive" moment
Tab 1 dead time (empty court 0:00-0:10)3x to 5xCompress dead time while keeping recovery clock visibly initializing

8. Freeze-frames + the lag pitfall

Critical lesson from yesterday

Hard freeze-frames stop the entire clip — including any animated UI elements (canvas, tickertape, signal bars). To an engineering judge, a frozen UI looks like the dashboard crashed. AVOID hard freezes on dashboard footage.

Use these alternatives instead:

  • 20% slow-mo via Speed Curve (right-click → Speed → Curve) — gives 5x reading time but UI stays "alive"
  • Callout-paired hard freeze — if you must hard-freeze, layer a CapCut text callout at the EXACT freeze moment so it reads as editorial choice, not browser crash

How to add a 20% slow-mo at a specific moment:

  1. Split the clip just before the moment you want to slow
  2. Split again just after the moment
  3. Click the middle piece
  4. Speed → Custom → 0.2x
  5. OR use Speed → Curve → drag the curve down for an eased slow-down

9. Muting OBS audio (CRITICAL)

Don't skip this

Every OBS capture has an AAC stereo audio track. Likely it captured your room noise / mouse clicks / breathing. If you don't mute these tracks, they'll compete with your music bed and make the demo sound amateur.

For EACH of your OBS captures (Tab 1, Tab 2, Tab 3, full_demo_all_tabs):

  1. Click the clip on the timeline
  2. Right-click → Mute (or Detach Audio then delete the audio sub-track)
  3. Verify: the clip's audio waveform should disappear from view, OR a small "muted" icon should appear on the clip
Exception — keep this audio

The Intro_typing_v1.mov clip's audio IS the asset — it's the keyboard clacks captured by your MacBook mic. Do NOT mute that clip. Keep it audible at around -16 dBFS, with the music ducked to -20 dBFS underneath during 0:00-0:22.

10. Text overlays (countdown + value-prop labels)

For your countdown vision (3-2-1) and any "5 days, 7 signals, Apple Silicon" value-prop labels:

  1. Click Text in the left sidebar
  2. Choose a basic text style (avoid the cartoonish ones — pick clean serif or mono)
  3. Drag onto Track 4 at the timeline position you want it
  4. Double-click the text on the Preview to edit content
  5. In Properties (right): set font, size, color, position

For the countdown, recommended specs:

  • Font: Fraunces (matches closing card) or system serif fallback (Georgia, Times)
  • Size: 180-220pt (massive — fills the screen)
  • Color: #F8FAFC (white) on solid black background
  • Position: dead center
  • Each number: 1.5 seconds long, hard cut between numbers
  • Optional: tiny scale-in animation (start at 80%, scale to 100% over 0.3s) for energy

For value-prop labels (e.g., "7 BIOMECHANICAL SIGNALS"):

  • Font: JetBrains Mono uppercase
  • Size: 24-32pt
  • Color: cyan #00E5FF on transparent
  • Position: bottom-third or top-third
  • Duration: 2-3 seconds
  • Animation: opacity fade-in 0.4s, fade-out 0.4s

11. Music track placement + keyframed swells

  1. Drag your music file from Media Bin onto Track 2
  2. Position at 0:00
  3. Trim the END to land at 3:00 + ~1 second of decay (so the final chord rings out into 1-2s of silence after the YouTube cut)
  4. Set base volume to -14 dBFS:
    • Click the music clip
    • Properties → Volume slider → drag to about 70% (rough -14 dBFS equivalent)
    • OR enter exact value if Properties has a numeric field

Keyframed editorial swells (this is the polish that elevates the demo):

At each pivot point in your demo, the music should briefly swell up by 1-3 dB to punctuate the visual transition. CapCut keyframes the volume:

  1. Click the music clip
  2. Properties → Volume → click the diamond/keyframe icon next to the volume slider (this enables keyframing)
  3. Position the timeline cursor at the moment you want the swell to BEGIN (e.g., 0:30 for the dashboard reveal)
  4. Set volume to current base (-14)
  5. Move cursor 0.5 seconds forward (to 0:30.5)
  6. Set volume to swell level (-12 or -11)
  7. Move cursor 1.5 seconds forward (to 0:32)
  8. Set volume back to base (-14)

Result: smooth volume swell from -14 to -12 over 0.5s, hold at -12 for 1.5s, return to -14. Repeat for each editorial pivot:

TimePivotSwell amount
0:30Dashboard reveal (after typing+terminal opener)+2 dB
1:50Tab 2 reveal (CSV download moment)+1 dB
2:15Tab 3 reveal (swarm)+2 dB
2:48Closing card entry+3 dB (sustain through closing)

12. Export settings + YouTube prep

  1. Top-right: Export button
  2. Settings:
    • Format: MP4
    • Resolution: 1920 × 1080
    • Frame Rate: 60 fps (or 30 if your project is 30)
    • Bitrate: 12 Mbps (CBR) — visually lossless at 1080p
    • Codec: H.264 (NOT H.265/HEVC — YouTube prefers H.264)
    • Audio Codec: AAC, 320 kbps, 48 kHz, stereo
    • Color Space: Rec. 709 / sRGB
  3. Output filename: panopticon_live_demo_v1_FINAL_2026-04-26.mp4
  4. Output location: ~/Documents/Panopticon_Final/
  5. Click Export. CapCut renders for 1-3 minutes (Apple Silicon hardware encoder is fast).
  6. When complete, OPEN IN QUICKTIME and watch end-to-end ONCE without scrubbing. QA audit (see below).

QA audit checklist (before YouTube upload)

  • [ ] Total runtime is 3:00 ± 1 second
  • [ ] No black flashes between cuts
  • [ ] No audio clipping (peaks under -3 dBFS)
  • [ ] Cyan reads as cyan (#00E5FF), not blue
  • [ ] Closing card holds for full 12 seconds
  • [ ] First frame is intentional (countdown 3 OR typing intro), not a black frame
  • [ ] Last frame is the closing card or 1s of black
  • [ ] No browser chrome / toolbar visible in OBS captures

14. When CapCut breaks (universal troubleshooting)

"My export looks different than the preview"

Almost always: a track is muted in preview but unmuted in export, or vice versa. Check the eye-icon and speaker-icon on each track header. Confirm what you intend.

"There's a black flash between two clips"

1-frame gap in the timeline. Zoom in (Cmd++) on the timeline at that point until you see the gap. Drag the right clip leftward until it snaps to the left clip's edge.

"My music keeps clipping/distorting"

Lower the music's base volume. -14 dBFS is the recommendation; if it sounds clipped at -14, lower to -16 or -18. The music doesn't need to be loud — it needs to be PRESENT.

"Speed-ramping made my clip blurry"

This is correct behavior at high speeds (10x+). The motion blur IS the visual signature for terminal-log-scrolling. If you don't want blur, use a lower speed multiplier (2-4x).

"My text overlay disappears at certain points"

The text clip has a duration too. Click it and check the right edge — drag it longer if needed. Text overlays don't auto-extend.

"CapCut crashed mid-edit"

CapCut auto-saves every 60-120 seconds. Re-open the project — you'll be back within ~1 minute of work. Save manually Cmd+S after every major operation as insurance.

"My preview is laggy / choppy"

Preview is real-time RENDER, not the export quality. Choppy preview is OK — the export is what matters. To make preview smoother: lower preview quality in CapCut settings (1/4 res preview is plenty for editing decisions). The exported file will be full quality regardless.

"I accidentally deleted something important"

Cmd+Z to undo. CapCut keeps a long undo history (often 100+ steps). Don't panic.

"YouTube upload says my file is invalid"

Codec mismatch. Re-export with H.264 (NOT H.265/HEVC). YouTube does accept HEVC but rejects some encoder variants.
